# Prototyping: Rapid Prototyping and Interaction Validation

Prototyping is for "low-cost learning."

## Checklist

1. **Select Fidelity** — Paper prototypes, low-fidelity wireframes, or interactive high-fidelity models?
2. **Define the Golden Path** — Ensure the prototype covers the shortest path for a user to complete a task.
3. **Integrate Visual Companion** — Use `skills/brainstorming/scripts/` rendering logic.
4. **Perform Prototype Testing** — Invite real users to click through and observe their points of confusion.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Over-polishing**: Spending hours adjusting shadows and rounded corners before validating the logic.
- **Ignoring Interaction Feedback**: Buttons in the prototype that provide no response when clicked.
- **Replacing the Spec**: Never assume that "looking at the pictures is enough to understand the logic." Prototypes must be used in conjunction with the Spec.
