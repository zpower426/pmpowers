# Negotiation & Conflict Resolution: Interests and Conflict Resolution

Negotiation is not about winning or losing, but about finding a third possibility.

## Checklist

1. **Identify Underlying Needs (Interests vs. Positions)** — Why does the other party insist on that solution? What do they really want?
2. **Prepare Alternatives (BATNA)** — If negotiations break down, what is our bottom line and alternative plan?
3. **Apply Principled Negotiation** — Set aside emotions and discuss based on objective criteria (e.g., metrics, cost, timeline).
4. **Record Consensus (The Deal)** — Immediately confirm reached agreements in writing after the negotiation.

## Expert Principles

- **Empathy**: Try to write the other party's "victory speech" and design your proposal accordingly.
- **Separate People from Problems**: Be tough on the issue, but soft on the person.
- **Seek Incremental Gains**: Look for "bargaining chips" that are low cost to you but high value to the other party.
