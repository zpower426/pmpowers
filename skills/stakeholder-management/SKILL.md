# Stakeholder Management: Stakeholder Alignment Guide

The Product Manager is the balancer of interests.

## Checklist

1. **Identify Stakeholders (Mapping)** — R&D, Sales, Marketing, Senior Management, Customers.
2. **Apply Power/Interest Grid** — Identify who needs to be managed closely versus who just needs to be kept informed.
3. **Develop Communication Strategy** — Adopt different communication frequencies and granularities for different roles.
4. **Get Early Buy-in** — Conduct one-on-one "socializing" before formal meetings.

## Core Framework: Power/Interest Grid

- **High Power / High Interest**: Key players, must be deeply involved throughout the process.
- **High Power / Low Interest**: Keep satisfied, they are potential obstacles if unhappy.
- **Low Power / High Interest**: Keep informed, they are often the best testers and advocates.
- **Low Power / Low Interest**: Monitor only, avoid wasting excessive energy on them.

## Expert Principles

- **No Surprises**: If a stakeholder hears about a major negative change for the first time in a review meeting, your communication has failed.
- **Interest Exchange**: Understand the other party's KPIs. They will only support you if you help them achieve their goals.
