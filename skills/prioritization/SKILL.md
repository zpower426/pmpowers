# Prioritization: Priority Decision-making and Weight Balancing

The core job of a PM is to say "No."

## Checklist

1. **Gather All Backlog Items (Intake)** — Transform vague requirements into concrete User Stories.
2. **Gather All Backlog Items (Intake)** — Transform vague requirements into concrete User Stories.
3. **Apply Evaluation Frameworks (RICE/Kano)** — Score each item.
4. **Assess Resource Constraints** — Development capacity, design resources, and time windows.
5. **Decide and Publicize (Communicate)** — Clearly define priorities 1, 2, 3, and explain the reasoning.

## Core Framework: RICE Scoring

| Dimension | Definition | Scoring |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Reach** | How many users will be affected in a quarter? | (Specific value) |
| **Impact** | How much does it contribute to the goal? | 0.25 (Minimal) - 3 (Massive) |
| **Confidence** | How strong is your data/evidence? | 50% - 100% |
| **Effort** | How many person-months are required? | (Number of person-months) |

**Formula: Score = (R * I * C) / E**

## Expert Principles

- **Don't Worship Formulas**: RICE is a tool to assist decision-making, not the final decision-maker.
- **Distinguish "Important" from "Urgent"**: Beware of urgent tasks that scream loudly but contribute nothing to North Star Metrics.
